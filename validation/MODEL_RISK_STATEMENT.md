# Model Risk Statement

## Required statement

AI Procurement Copilot is a decision-support and portfolio demonstration system. It does not autonomously approve suppliers, execute awards, replace due diligence, or substitute for commercial, legal, quality, financial, compliance, sustainability, or executive review.

## Intended use

- Demonstrate transparent procurement decision logic.
- Compare supplier bids using should-cost, TCO, risk, performance, ESG and relationship factors.
- Support scenario analysis, negotiation preparation and executive communication.
- Surface data gaps, assumptions, defaults, risks and required human approvals.

## Prohibited use

- Autonomous supplier approval, award, contract execution or payment.
- Bankruptcy prediction, audited financial analysis or credit certification.
- ESG certification, sanctions screening or legal/compliance approval.
- Use of confidential supplier data without authorization.
- Production decisions based only on synthetic, defaulted or unverified data.

## Data requirements

A defensible recommendation requires valid supplier identity, positive quoted price, explicit currency and unit, MOQ, lead time, payment terms, incoterms, annual volume, feasible capacity, and sufficient risk and performance evidence. Category-specific fields improve credibility.

## Defaulting policy

Defaults exist to keep portfolio demonstrations operational. Every defaulted field must be visible, included in the data-confidence score and replaced by verified data for production use. Defaults must never be described as supplier facts.

## Human approval

Human approval is mandatory for every recommendation, allocation, classification, negotiation position, supplier-development plan and executive narrative.

## Financial disclaimer

Financial health outputs are indicators derived from available operational and dependency fields. They are not audited financial statements, ratings, insolvency forecasts or bankruptcy probabilities.

## ESG disclaimer

ESG outputs are maturity indicators only. Verification requires current certifications, audit evidence, emissions data, labor and human-rights documentation, governance controls and sustainability commitments.

## Data-confidence meaning

Data confidence measures completeness and reliance on defaults, missing fields and inferences. It does not measure truthfulness and is not the probability that the recommendation is correct.

## Recommendation-confidence meaning

Recommendation confidence reflects separation between modeled alternatives. It is not statistical confidence, forecast accuracy or probability of supplier success.

## External-data limitations

The current portfolio build does not use live commodity indices, FX feeds, supplier financial statements, sanctions data, country risk, logistics disruptions, weather, quality systems, contracts or verified ERP supplier masters.

## Production-readiness statement

The system is suitable for portfolio demonstration and controlled testing. Production use would require verified integrations, security and privacy controls, access management, audit logging, model ownership, calibration, change control, independent validation and approved procurement governance.
