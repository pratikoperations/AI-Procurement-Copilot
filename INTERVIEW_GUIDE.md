# Interview Guide

## Project Positioning

AI Procurement Copilot demonstrates how category-aware procurement intelligence can improve sourcing quality, supplier governance, risk visibility, decision transparency, and executive communication while keeping currency, evidence, validation, and human approval explicit.

## Interview Pitch

The project converts RFQ and supplier data into category-specific should-cost, TCO, risk, allocation, negotiation, Procurement Intelligence, and Supplier 360 outputs. RC1.2 adds auditable original-to-normalized currency handling, category-aware supplier communication, governed recommendation roles, classification precedence, and consistent business-readable exports.

It does not simply select the lowest price, silently convert currencies, force unsupported supplier awards, or hide weak evidence behind strong scores.

## Recommended Demo Flow

1. Select Packaging Procurement or Raw Material Procurement.
2. Load synthetic demo data or upload an RFQ.
3. Show Original Currency, Normalized Currency, FX Rate Used, Unit of Measure, and Comparison Basis in the readable report.
4. Open the Validation Assurance Gate.
5. Explain data confidence and eligibility status.
6. Demonstrate lowest price versus best value.
7. Show category-specific should-cost and TCO.
8. Open Supplier Intelligence and explain governed Financial, ESG, and Innovation scores.
9. Show `No Qualified Supplier` for roles where evidence is insufficient.
10. Open Executive Outputs and compare Packaging versus PET Resin supplier communication.
11. Open Downloads and explain the difference between readable reports and machine-readable audit data.
12. Demonstrate that blocked status is consistent across screen, memo, email, narrative, and audit output.
13. Close with human approval and model-risk limits.

## Strong Interview Answer

> I built an AI-enabled procurement decision-support platform that separates original supplier quotations from normalized comparison data. It records currency, FX rate, unit of measure, and comparison basis; applies category-specific should-cost and TCO logic; and then governs recommendations using data confidence, evidence completeness, business rules, and human approval. The system can refuse to assign sustainability, innovation, strategic-partner, or long-term roles when evidence is insufficient. It also generates category-aware supplier communication and keeps business-readable reports separate from machine-readable audit data. The goal is not autonomous award approval; it is transparent, defensible procurement decision support.

## Validation Talking Points

- Green CI proves expected code paths, not business correctness.
- Original and normalized prices are separated.
- Unsupported currency conversion is blocked.
- PET Resin uses kg.
- Risk Resilience Score means higher is better.
- Data confidence measures completeness, not probability of correctness.
- Recommendation roles use governed displayed scores and evidence status.
- Exit Candidate precedence prevents contradictory long-term or strategic roles.
- Blocked status must match across screen and downloads.
- Financial health is an indicator, not bankruptcy prediction.
- Human procurement approval remains mandatory.

## Business Value

- Reduces lowest-price bias and false confidence.
- Prevents invalid currency and unit comparisons.
- Improves supplier clarification quality by category.
- Prevents unsupported sustainability, innovation, and strategic claims.
- Creates auditable and executive-readable sourcing outputs.
- Supports negotiation, supplier development, continuity, and SRM planning.
